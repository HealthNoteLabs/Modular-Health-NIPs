The Problem

Health data is the most personal data there is — and the most locked up.

Open your phone's health app and try to export every metric it holds — not a screenshot, the structured data — into a file another app can read natively. You can't. The data is visible to you and owned by them. That's not a bug in one company. It's the silo model itself: closed, extractive, and structurally opposed to the interest of the person generating the data.

01 / Ownership

You hold a view, not the asset

Your data sits in someone else's database under someone else's terms. On-device isn't the same as owned — leave the ecosystem and your history doesn't come with you.

02 / Interoperability

The apps don't talk

Your running app, sleep app, and scale each hold a fragment. Assembling the whole picture needs yet another company in the middle that harvests it as the price of integration.

03 / Privacy

You are the product

The business model of "free" health tracking is data. Conditions, cycles, mental-health signals — the things you'd never post — become an asset on someone else's books.

The fix has to be architectural. No better-behaved custodian solves a problem that is the architecture. The data has to belong to the person by construction — which means the data layer can't be owned by whoever builds the app.

NIP-101h Protocol

An open standard for health metrics on Nostr.

Nostr gives you signed, portable events. NIP-101h gives two different apps a shared language for what a "weight" event looks like, what unit it carries, and how it's encrypted. That's what makes the data actually interoperable instead of silos on a shared network.

  • Modular by design

    Each metric is its own micro-spec on its own event kind. Publish your weight without ever touching your step count — reveal exactly what you choose, metric by metric.

  • Encrypted by default, not by setting

    Clients encrypt the value of every metric with NIP-44 before it leaves the device. Privacy is on unless you turn it off — never the reverse. Only you hold the keys to read it.

  • Charts from tags, values stay private

    Non-sensitive tags (unit, timestamp) stay readable so an app can build and sort your timeline — then decrypt the actual values locally, in your client, with your key.

Sovereign Stack

Your data. Your relay. Your AI. Your node.

The default path hands your health data to a cloud AI to process on someone else's machine — the exact problem this project exists to solve. NIP-101h data doesn't have to work that way. About

A health project that found the right tools — not a crypto project that wandered into health.

Most projects in this space are crypto projects that decided health looked like a large market. Health Note Labs is the inverse. The founder's background is in behavioral health — direct work in Applied Behavior Analysis for autism therapy and time inside mental-health facilities. That's hands-on work with some of the most sensitive health data and most vulnerable people there are, inside the systems meant to serve them.

ABA is an intensely data-driven discipline — granular measurement, accurate provenance, longitudinal continuity, sharing specific data with specific people on the client's terms. Those requirements are visible all over NIP-101h. The motivation is lived, not theoretical: having watched the systems that hold people's most sensitive data fail them, and concluding the fix had to be architectural.

“This isn't a crypto project that decided to do health. It's a health professional who chose the right technology — and stays disciplined about what it does not claim.”

Health Note Labs builds infrastructure for owning and moving health data. It is not a medical device, it does not diagnose, and it makes no treatment claims.

  • Shipping, not slideware

    RUNSTR is live and paying Bitcoin. NIP-101h is published with 14+ metrics. The SDK, API, and relay are specified and in build.

  • Privacy you can verify

    Open standard, open formats, client-side encryption. The claims are checkable in code, not taken on faith.

  • The win condition is the standard

    An ecosystem where most apps aren't ours. A developer who builds a better app on NIP-101h is the ecosystem working as designed.
